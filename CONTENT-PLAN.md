# Thinkerbell — Content Backlog

The spine of the blog. Every post here is tagged with its section, its prerequisites, and the diagram that goes with it.

**The loop:** pick the next unchecked box → write it → commit → tick the box.

**The rule that makes it work:** never write a payoff post (comparison, applied essay) before its foundations exist. The foundations are what make the payoff *earned* rather than a hot take.

---

## How to tag every post

Put these in the front matter of every post, from day one. Minima ignores them today, but the moment you switch to a theme with categories, your whole archive snaps into a browsable grid — retroactively.

```yaml
---
layout: post
title: "Your Title"
date: YYYY-MM-DD
categories: [tradition, era]     # western|indian|chinese  ×  ancient|medieval|early-modern|modern|contemporary
tags: [thinker, concept, branch] # branch = metaphysics|epistemology|ethics|political|aesthetics
---
```

## Post types (the six primitives)

| Type | What it is | Signature diagram |
|---|---|---|
| **Era overview** | A whole period in 10 minutes | Timeline strip |
| **Thinker profile** | One philosopher, one big idea | Influence tree (ancestors → them → descendants) |
| **Concept deep-dive** | A single idea, taken apart | Argument map |
| **Primary text** | Close-read one passage | Annotated quote breakdown |
| **Comparison** | One question, several traditions | Side-by-side table |
| **Applied essay** | Philosophy meets now | Whatever the argument needs |

## Two things that stay yours

The drafting can be fast and assisted. These two can't be, or the learning leaks out:

1. **Draw the concept map by hand first.** The act of arranging the boxes is where understanding actually forms. Then have the clean version made.
2. **End every post with one question you genuinely cannot answer.** It's reader engagement and self-test in one. If you can't find one, you haven't understood the topic deeply enough yet — go back.
